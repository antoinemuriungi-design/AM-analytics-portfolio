Project: Apiculture (Meru County, Kenya)

This project analyzes the determinants of income among small-scale beekeepers using survey data. The analysis focuses on the role of training, technology adoption, and education in influencing income outcomes.

Methods Used:

·         Descriptive statistics

·         Chi-square and Fisher’s Exact Test

·         Ordered logistic regression

library(readxl)

Data Cleaning

# Fix income category
apiculture_data$ANNUALINCOME[
  apiculture_data$ANNUALINCOME == "100001-"
] <- "100001+"

# Convert to ordered factor
apiculture_data$ANNUALINCOME <- factor(
  apiculture_data$ANNUALINCOME,
  levels = c("<5000", "5000-10000", "11000-20000", "21000-100000", "100001+"),
  ordered = TRUE
)

# Convert predictors
apiculture_data$TYPEOFHIVES <- factor(apiculture_data$TYPEOFHIVES)
apiculture_data$FORMALBEEKEEPINGTRAINING <- factor(apiculture_data$FORMALBEEKEEPINGTRAINING)

Key Findings

  • Majority of beekeepers fall in low-income category
  • Training significantly improves income outcomes
  • Traditional hives reduce productivity
  • Education has a weaker effect compared to training
